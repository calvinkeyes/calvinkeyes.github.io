Projects

I got to work on a bunch of cool projects as an undergrad.

For one project, I explored instruction level parallelism using loop unrolling. The blue line represents an unrolled loop. We see about a 1.4x speedup over the baseline. This makes sense because we are getting rid of unnecessary comparison operations. However, we see a significant speed up when we interleave instructions that are independent of each other. This means we are better able to fill our data pipeline. We see a max of about 5.7x speedup when we carefully write out the code!

In another project, I designed my own six transistor SRAM cell. Throughout the quarter, we learned various electronic design automation (EDA) tools. Putting all of the tools together, I designed and tested the performance of an SRAM cell. The layout of my cell is below.
